Hybrid retrieval combines two search methods. Semantic search uses dense vector embeddings and wins on conceptual or paraphrased queries. BM25 is a lexical method that wins on exact identifiers, rare codes, and literal term matches. Because the two scores live on different scales, you cannot simply add them together. Reciprocal rank fusion merges the two ranked lists by rank position instead of by raw score. Each document gets a fused score equal to the sum over the lists of one divided by the constant k plus the rank. A document that ranks highly in both lists is rewarded the most. After fusion, an optional language model re-ranking pass can reorder the top candidates by true relevance. The final answer must cite the original chunk text so a reader can trace every claim back to its source.
